The Problem

The main problem we are trying to solve with our app is the lack of knowledge about Swine Flu in Jalisco, to make this possible we are going to give information about how to prevent it, how to know if you are sick, what to do if you are sick, if you feel bad or both. Jalisco has had many cases these past months. Over six hundred confirmed cases of Swine Flu of different ages. The worst mistake people had done is to confuse the Swine Flu with the common cold. Treating on with the other can end in death in either case.

Our Solution

We will try to give a solution to this problem creating an application that will share the knowledge of how to detect the Swine Flue, what to do if you or someone you know has it, where to go if medical attention is needed, and many more aspects about this disease that everyone must be aware of. This application will be simple and very usefull to everyone of any age and it will prepare the user to confront the Swine Flu.

Context

Jalisco in the state with the most cases of people with influenza, also known as "the flu". There has been over 618 reported cases and 77 deaths from the flu, that is why we will create an app to inform people about the flu, causes and symptoms, and include a way for people to contact hospitals and provide emergency numbers.

Audience

The audience that our app is trying to reach is people from Jalisco between 14 and 40 years, they are the active audience of the app because older people are not very used to smartphones and younger people are not interested in these subjects, they want to play games and have fun.

Symptoms Recognition

Flu symptoms include:

  • A 100°F or higher fever or feeling feverish (not everyone with the flu has a fever)
  • A cough and/or sore throat
  • A runny or stuffy nose
  • Headaches and/or body aches
  • Chills
  • Fatigue
  • Nausea, vomiting, and/or diarrhea (most common in children)

    • Where to go?

    If you feel bad inmediatly go to the nearest hospital to your location.

    What to do?

    Seek medical attention immediately if you experience any of the following:

  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Purple or blue discoloration of the lips
  • Pain or pressure in the chest or abdomen
  • Sudden dizziness
  • Confusion
  • Severe or persistent vomiting
  • Seizures
  • Flu-like symptoms that improve but then return with fever and worse cough
